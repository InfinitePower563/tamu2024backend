Emergency Funds

What is an emergency fund?

An emergency fund is a separate account that you keep that you dip into in case of an emergency. For example, if you get injured when you have no money, then you can dip into your emergency fund to help you pay for the healthcare costs.

How much money should I be putting in my emergency fund?

According to Vanguard, an emergency fund should last at least 3-6 months of living expenses. You realistically don’t need to put that much money into your emergency fund. A good rule of thumb is to take your disposable income (amount of money left after taxes and cost of living expenses), and put 30% of it into your emergency fund and 30% into your savings. If you also invest your emergency fund into high yield savings, then you can also earn a little extra money on the fund as well.

What should I use my emergency fund for?

Please note that these lists are not exhaustive. Use your own judgement for determining whether a situation warrants the use of your emergency fund.

Do:

  • Unexpected Job Layoff/Firing
    • If you get laid off or fired from your job, then your emergency fund can help you pay your cost of living expenses until you get a new one.
  • Medical Emergencies
    • If you get injured with no money, then you have a way to pay off the medical expenses.
  • Car Repairs
    • If your car suddenly breaks down, then you should use your emergency fund to pay the repair costs.
  • Home Repairs
    • If you experience a natural disaster (like an earthquake or flood) then your emergency fund can help pay repair costs.
  • Family Emergencies
    • You can use your emergency fund to pay off unexpected travel or support expenses.

Don't:

  • Video Games
    • Don’t spend your emergency fund on non-essential purchases.
  • Vacations
    • Don’t spend your emergency fund on leisure activities, that’s what your savings are for.
  • Routine Monthly Expenses
    • If you need to spend your emergency fund on your rent, bills, or other cost of living expenses, and you have a stable source of income, then you should budget better.
  • Home Upgrades
    • You should be spending your savings on non-essential home upgrades.
  • Pet Expenses
    • Unless your pet is dying, then you shouldn’t be using your emergency fund to purchase more toys for your cute little kitty.
